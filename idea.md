# Your Plan: S.N.S (Swillnspin) — Car Care & Protection, Online

## What You're Creating

A website for S.N.S that lets any car owner in Alexandria — whether they drive a modest sedan or a high-end SUV — browse your full menu of car wash, detailing, coating, tinting, and paint protection film (PPF) services, see clear pricing for their exact vehicle size, and book a time slot online. Behind the scenes, you and your team get a dashboard to manage every product, booking, and customer without digging through WhatsApp chats and notebooks.

## Why This Is Worth Doing

Right now, someone interested in S.N.S has to message you on WhatsApp or Facebook, wait for a reply, and go back and forth just to find out a price. A website removes that friction — customers see everything upfront, book instantly, and trust you more because you look like an established, professional operation. For you, it means fewer repetitive messages, an organized record of every customer and job, and a brand that looks as premium as the work you actually do.

---

## What This Will Look Like When It's Done

A visitor lands on a sleek, dark, chrome-and-blue website that immediately feels like a high-end garage — the same feeling your logo already gives off. They pick their vehicle type (sedan, SUV, or truck/van), and every price on the page updates to match. They browse categories: Wash, Detailing, Ceramic Coating, PPF, Tinting. For PPF, they notice they can either pay the full amount or split it into installments. They pick a date and time slot on a calendar, choose whether they'll drive in or want pickup/drop-off, and confirm — paying now or on the day, whichever they prefer. Before leaving, they scroll through a gallery of stunning before/after transformations and read reviews from real customers.

Meanwhile, you or your staff log into a separate dashboard where every booking of the day is listed, every customer's history is on file, and you can add a new service, change a price, or mark a job as complete in a couple of clicks.

---

## What It Will Do (The Features, In Plain English)

### Browse Services by Vehicle Type
A customer picks their vehicle size — small (sedan), medium (SUV), or large (truck/van) — and every price across the whole site adjusts to match. No confusion, no "starting from" prices that turn out to be wrong for their car.

### Full Service Menu
Customers explore everything you offer in clear categories — car wash packages, interior detailing, ceramic coating, paint protection film, and window tinting — each with a description, photos, and a transparent price for their vehicle size.

### Book a Time Slot
Instead of messaging you to ask "when are you free?", the customer picks an open slot from a live calendar, so both sides know exactly when the car is expected — no back and forth.

### Choose Drop-In or Pickup/Drop-off
At checkout, the customer decides whether they'll bring the car to the Smouha shop themselves, or want your team to collect and return it — matching how you actually operate today.

### Pay However Suits Them
Whether it's cash on the day, or paying digitally when the car is delivered back to them, the customer isn't forced into paying online in advance if they don't want to.

### Split PPF Into Installments
For the paint protection film jobs (which are naturally the biggest tickets), a customer can choose to pay over a few installments instead of the full amount at once — making the higher-end protection more approachable without turning it into a subscription.

### See the Proof — Gallery & Reviews
Before/after photos of real cars you've worked on sit right on the site, alongside reviews from past customers — giving new visitors real confidence before they ever call you.

### Everything Managed From One Dashboard
You and your staff log into a private area where you can add or edit services and prices, see every booking on a calendar, look up a customer's past visits, and track which jobs are upcoming, in progress, or done — replacing scattered notes and chat threads with one clear system.

### Staff Access, Kept Organized
Because it's not just you running things, the dashboard supports a couple of staff logins (like a receptionist handling bookings and a technician marking jobs complete) without giving everyone access to everything.

## Branding Notes (Carried Into the Website)

- **Look & Feel:** Premium and professional — a "luxury garage" feeling throughout, using the same dark background, brushed-steel, and blue-chrome accents as your logo.
- **Logo Usage:** Your current logo is a full illustrated banner (the technician, the car, the shield). For the website we'll need two adapted versions:
  - A simplified **icon/favicon** version — likely just the shield emblem or the "S.N.S" lettering on its own, since the full illustration is too detailed to read at a tiny tab-icon size.
  - A **header/top-bar version** — a cropped or simplified horizontal lockup (just the "S.N.S" wordmark plus tagline, or wordmark plus a small emblem) that stays legible and clean in a slim navigation bar, rather than squeezing the entire illustrated scene in there.
- **Tagline:** "Care. Shine. Defend." carries through as the brand's voice — used in the homepage headline and possibly repeated in section headers.
- **Tone of voice:** Confident and precise, not overly casual — the language of a shop that knows exactly what it's doing with your paint.

---

## Things to Keep in Mind

- **Pricing by vehicle size adds real value, but needs upkeep:** since every service now has 3 prices instead of 1, keeping them accurate and up to date across the whole menu will take a bit of discipline in the dashboard.
- **Installments need a simple rule:** decide early on things like how many installments are allowed, and what happens if a customer books installments and then doesn't show up — this can be a simple internal policy, not something visitors need to see in detail.
- **Pickup/drop-off adds logistics, not just a website toggle:** the site can capture the customer's request, but you'll still need your own way (even a simple one) to track which cars are out for pickup versus sitting at the shop.
- **Reviews are powerful but need moderation:** letting customers leave reviews builds trust fast, but you'll want a simple way to approve what gets shown publicly.
